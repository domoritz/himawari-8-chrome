Here you can choose the image source.

The Japanese Himawari 8 Satellite takes images of different parts of the spectrum (visible light and infrared). Himawari offers the best image quality but the satellite is geostationary.

You can also choose images from the NASA's EPIC camera on board of the DSCOVR satellite. This satellite always faces the sunlit side of the Earth.

The NOAA GOES satellites are geostationary like Himawari but show the pacific (GOES West) and the Americas (GOES East).

The Meteosat satellites are geostationary as well, but the Meteosat Prime satellite is centered at 0°E, while the Indian Ocean Data Coverage (IODC) is centered at 41.5°E41.5°E41.5°E41.5°E.
